# The Digital Twin (Gazebo & Unity)

Welcome to the Digital Twin module! This educational resource introduces digital twin environments for humanoid robots using physics simulation and high-fidelity rendering.

## Overview

This module is designed for CS/AI students familiar with basic ROS 2 concepts. You'll learn about:

- **Physics Simulation with Gazebo**: Understanding digital twin concepts, gravity, collisions, physics engines, and simulating humanoid motion
- **Environment & Interaction in Unity**: Exploring high-fidelity rendering, human-robot interaction concepts, and the balance between visual realism and physical accuracy
- **Sensor Simulation**: Learning about simulated sensors including LiDAR, depth cameras, IMUs, and sensor data flow to control systems

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

- Explain the concept and role of digital twins in robotics
- Understand physics simulation fundamentals including gravity, collisions, and physics engines
- Describe how humanoid motion is simulated in physics environments
- Compare high-fidelity rendering in Unity with physical accuracy requirements
- Identify different types of simulated sensors and their applications
- Explain how sensor data flows to control systems in digital twin environments

## Prerequisites

- Basic understanding of ROS 2 concepts
- Fundamental knowledge of robotics principles
- Familiarity with simulation concepts

Let's begin exploring how digital twin environments enable the development and testing of humanoid robots!
